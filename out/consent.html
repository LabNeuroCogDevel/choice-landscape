ONLINE CONSENT FORM

We are researchers at the University of Pittsburgh doing a research study about learning and decision making. To accomplish this, we are asking participants to complete a brief web-based game and to answer a few short questions. The game involves moving an animated character around your screen to learn where rewarding items are located. Questions presented at the end include basic demographic information (for example, your age), feedback about the task (for example, whether you found it fun or confusing), and information about how you completed the task (for example, what sort of device you used to respond during the task). Participation should take under 15 minutes and is completely voluntary.

What are the possible risks, side effects, and discomforts of this research study?

Your participation in this study does not involve any risk to you beyond that of everyday life. Taking part in this research study may not benefit you personally, but we may learn new things that could help others.

If you have questions or concerns about the study, you can contact the researchers by contacting Dr. Beatriz Luna at (412) 383-8167.

If you have any questions about your rights as a participant in this research, feel you have been harmed, or wish to discuss other study-related concerns with someone who is not part of the research team, you can contact the University of Pittsburgh Human Research Protection Office (HRPO) by phone at (412) 383-1480, or by email at irb.reliance@pitt.edu.

Confidentiality Statement

Who will have access to identifiable information related to my participation in this research study?

Your Mechanical Turk Worker ID will be used to distribute payment to you but will not be stored with the research data we collect from you. Please be aware that your MTurk Worker ID can potentially be linked to information about you on your Amazon public profile page, depending on the settings you have for your Amazon profile. We will not be accessing any personally identifying information about you that you may have put on your Amazon public profile page. If you decide to withdraw, data collected up until the point of withdrawal may still be included in analysis.

In addition to the approved research staff, the following individuals will or may have access to identifiable information (which may include your identifiable research record information) related to your participation in this research study: Authorized representatives of the University of Pittsburgh Office of Research Protections, may review your identifiable research information for the purpose of monitoring the appropriate conduct of this research study.

We might use your research data in future studies. These future studies might be done by us or by other investigators. Before we use your data, we will remove any potentially identifiable information. There still may be a chance that someone could figure out that the information is about you. Per University of Pittsburgh policy, all research records will be maintained for at least 7 years following final reporting or publication of a project.

Will I be paid for taking part in this research study?

Participant(s) that fully complete the online follow-up survey battery will receive $2.50, based on the expected time to complete the task (15min). Note that MTurk does not allow for prorated compensation. In the event of an incomplete HIT, you must contact the research team and compensation will be determined based on what was completed and at the researchers' discretion.

PLEASE NOTE: This study contains attention checks to make sure that participants are finishing the tasks honestly and completely. As long as you read the instructions and complete the tasks, your HIT will be approved. If you fail these checks, your HIT will be rejected.

Is my participation in this research study voluntary?

Your participation in this research study, to include the use and disclosure of information for the purposes described above, is completely voluntary. (Note, however, that if you do not provide your consent for the use and disclosure of your provided information for the purposes described above, you will not be allowed to participate in the research study.) Whether or not you provide your consent for participation in this research study will have no effect on your current or future relationship with the University of Pittsburgh. Whether or not you provide your consent for participation in this research study will have no effect on your current or future medical care at the UPMC or affiliated health care provider or your current or future relationship with a health care insurance provider.
